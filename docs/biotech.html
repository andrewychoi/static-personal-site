The next decades will see great leaps in biotech. This sector is the place where the greatest opportunities are for people who want to start companies.

Counterexamples

However, the widespread availability of this sequencing data has not led to massive throughput in DNA-based diagnosis of disease. Platforms → Infrastructure

In the software world of the mid 00s, the cloud revolution was taking place. Previously, in the 90s, it was an extraordinary ordeal to build a software company, with a requirement to order hardware to run your own software and link it to the internet. Gallons of ink and keystrokes have been spilled explaining why servers and the cloud have dominated the way that we ship technology through the 2010s and today, but one of the key aspects must be that the increased trust that customers had in software and services delivered through the internet. Consequentially, one of the ways that incumbents would lose to startups was that they were slower to adopt this technology that allowed them to move faster.

It looks like this pattern might repeat in the bio world. Incumbents spend a lot of time and capital to set up large labs staffed with dutiful research scientists.

In a world in which new platforms show up on a regular basis and change the way that life science research and applications are built, then there will be opportunities to build infrastructure businesses.

Infrastructure → Platform → Application

The question is either “how do we know which platforms will end up succeeding” or “what infrastructure is platform-independent”.

Software → Bio

Marc Andreesen famously claimed that software would eat the world in 2010, while his VC firm, a16z, followed suit by claiming that biology would eat the world in 2019. However, in the intervening 3 years we haven’t seen the world that they had claimed.

A common truism in startups is that good ideas only make good businesses if the timing is right. It is incredibly difficult (if not impossible) to ex ante determine that the timing is right, which means that we must rely on (primarily) luck, and (hopefully) intuition.

I am not the only software person that is trying to get into the biotech world–communities like Bits in Bio are satisfying this latent demand. There is some structural movement of people to new places, but for some reason this feels different.

Non-zero sum

Many applications in the biotech space are value-, health-, and wealth-creating ex nihilo. Creating a replacement for petroleum as a feedstock for chemical creation would be a creation of a new option without necessarily destroying the old. Cures for diseases do not necessarily mean that a new disease is caused in conjunction with the cure. This aspect makes the space much more attractive to work in.

Abstractions

Abstractions are one of the most useful concepts in computer science. For software people, abstractions go hand in hand with invariants: an abstraction is useful if it provides an invariant property for reasoning about the system that it concerns. However, software (and much of information technology) is designed from first principles such that the abstractions that we use to reason about the systems are sufficient. This is not necessarily the case for the life sciences. Many of the ideas that we hold about the way biology works are driven by convenience, rather than design.

A good example of this: “natural selection has taken organisms over many many years and provided a mechanism for perfecting things” is a paradigm that seems correct. However, natural selection is a high-level description of a process that may not actually act on the unit that matters. Protein sequence is fully determined by the DNA that generates the protein, and since mutations for an organism almost always happen at the genetic level, the resulting protein space that natural selection has (in addition to the stride length it can take over that space) is non-optimal. This leads to results in which rough stabs in the dark are likely to show meaningful improvements (e.g. deep learning trained on unstructured protein data being able to meaningfully improve protein function).
